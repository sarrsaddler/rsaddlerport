Designing for an Error State

UX Design
Client
IBM, Bluemine team
My Contributions
Duration: One day
During this project my primary job role was being the UX designer.
Project Overview
Bluemine is a web application created by IBM's Market Development and Insight organization (MD&I). Bluemine exists as a market place for aggregated market intelligence and research from subscription-based services for all IBMers to access. Bluemine channels allow all IBMers to contribute their point of view to different market topics, in an open space. My job was to address a gap in the experience where users who have not signed up and try to access content receive a blank webpage.
IBMers that haven't opted into using Bluemine receive a blank page when trying to access channel connect. Help patch this gap in the Bluemine channel's experience and provide IBMers who haven't opted in options.
Design Ask
Dictator
Challenges
I believe what made this project challenging was the lack of documentation on how to treat certain events within channels. I had to connect with other colleagues on the Bluemine team to gain clarity.
My process
What were my impediments during this project?
As a team, composed of my manager and a front end developer, we had a short turnaround time to come up with a solution.
  • Focus on the content first- I had to ask myself, what is the purpose of an error page? What information would I provide the user?
  • Look for inspiration - I created a mood board of 404 error pages to be inspired.
  • Leveraging Slack-  As I began to work through my ideas, I shared my wires with the team to gain feedback on the wording.
Final thoughts
I believe that Bluemine channels will eventually need a complete redesign because there are more opportunities for improvement within the experience.

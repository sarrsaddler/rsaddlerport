Bluemine Market View

UI/UX design
Client
IBM's Bluemine team
My Contributions
Three weeks (Nov 2019)
During this project my primary job role was being the UX designer.
Project Overview
Bluemine is a web application created by IBM's Market Development and Insight organization (MD&I). Bluemine exists as a market place for aggregated market intelligence and research from subscription-based services for all IBMers to access. Similar to Google search's user interface, the Bluemine product relies on its search feature to help users find the data that they need. MD&I's leadership on a semi-annual basis creates and publishes reports on Bluemine covering a specific segment called Market views. These Market views are often used by business unit leaders within IBM to develop their strategic plan to help IBM remain competitive among the rival companies. I was included in the small team MD&I senior leadership gathered to elevate those market views.

The initial design ask was to build a landing page on Bluemine that would house all the market views.

Challenges
What were my impediments during this project?
As a team composed of three designers and a software engineer we all felt that creating a landing page that would serve as a repository for all market views wasn't the best solution for this problem. After talking the project over as a team we decided to go against MD&I senior leadership request. This did create some challenges for us.
  • Market Views didn't have consistent names- I had to track down each market view team to verify the correct report.
  • Executives wanted a landing page - The MD&I leadership team were our stakeholders in this process, and they desired a landing page.
Team's Process
What were my pain points during this project?
As a team, we worked together to create a solution. We concluded that the minimum viable product of elevating the market view reports was through Bluemine's search results because of the way users interact with the platform. We envision that if a user queried a vague term like Cloud or included the word market view that we should elevate the most relevant market view in their search results.  If the search weren't correct, there would be a call to action that directs the user to a Bluemine report that contains the links to each market view. We relied on agile practices and mocked flow up as quickly as possible. Here are the steps we took to come to our design recommendation.
  1. Relied heavily on Bluemine user research- Bluemine has user data to showcase how people are interacting with the tool. We found out that almost 85% of Bluemine users interact with the search to navigate the product.
  2. Staying agile -  As a team, we leverage daily scrum meetings to provide updates, Github/zenhub to track the design process, and also set up a time to have a design critique. 
  3. Gained executive support- A week into the project, we presented to Bluemine's director to help us champion this idea to the executive leadership team. 
Design Impact
We were successful in change the minds of the MD&I's executive leadership team and developing an MVP. Due to our efforts within the search results of a user's query, market views will be elevated to the top of the page with the featured tag.
Final thoughts
There is still more work to be done on the market view solution. However, traction for this project dwindled around December 2019 due to other priorities of MD&I executive leadership team.

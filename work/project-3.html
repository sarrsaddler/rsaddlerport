Bilingual Email Campaign

E-mail marketing/Design
Client
IBM Bluemine
My Contributions
Two days (November 2019)
During this project my primary job role was being the UX designer.
Project Overview
The Market Development and Insight organization operate like IBM's internal consultant. The organization provides IBM The Market Development and Insight organization operate like IBM's internal consultant. The organization provides IBM senior leadership with competitor analysis. Bluemine, a product of MD&I, is a web application that serves IBMers as a market place for data, reports, and other research. Bluemine's enablement team frequently sends out to users their e-mail campaigns to promote product changes and increase usage of Bluemine. I was assigned to help the MDI's Japanese, Bluemine, vendor management team. Create an E-mail template that will be sent out to Bluemine's Japanese audience and also providing English speakers with guidance.
Design Prompt
What was I asked to do?
  • Help reduce churn (or retain Bluemine users) by devising an email template that will allow the Bluemine team to communicate its product value to a predominantly Japanese audience.
My process
How did I approach the design solution?
I took several steps to reach my design recommendation. Given my experience designing e-mail templates in the past, this assignment was a little easier for me.
  1. Content first- I reviewed the content to see where I could find the consistency.
  2. Schedule a call with all the stakeholders involved in the project. During this meeting, I was trying to figure the scope of the project, learn what was feasible in the tool, and manage expectations.
  3. Mocking up the new website- I create low fidelity - mid-fidelity wires to apply the knowledge. I decided to put a line of English above the Japanese content to indicate that the English translation was done below.
Challenges
What were my pain points during this project?
This was my first time designing something for a Japanese audience. However, due to the quick turn around time, I didn't let my gap in knowledge stop me.
  • Stakeholder time zone difference- I had to set up 8 pm calls to gain feedback and clarity with the stakeholder.
  • Content changes - I was not responsible for the content within the e-mail. I only had approval to layout the information in a way where it made sense to their audience.
Design impact
I was successful in building the template within the Acoustic tool. I also guide the Japanese team to put HTML attributes within their e-mails to indicate the language of the content. The e-mail went to 9300+ users within the Japanese audience and had an open rate of 63%. Ultimately, my efforts drove to help MDI's Japanese, Bluemine, vendor management team drive traffic to the Bluemine site.
Final thoughts
I thought that this was a fun design challenge.My design was limited to the capabilities of email so I had to rethink my approach which stretch me to think of a simplier strategy.
